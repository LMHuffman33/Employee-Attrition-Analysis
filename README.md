# Employee Attrition & Workforce Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview
This project uses Power BI to analyze employee attrition and workforce trends. The dashboard explores which employee groups have higher turnover risk based on department, job role, age, income, tenure, overtime, job satisfaction, and work-life balance.

The goal of this project is to identify patterns in employee attrition and present the findings through an interactive, business-focused Power BI dashboard.

## Business Question
What factors are associated with employee attrition, and which employee groups appear to have the highest turnover risk?

## Tools Used
- Power BI
- Power Query
- DAX
- CSV dataset

## Dataset
The dataset contains employee information such as:
- Age
- Department
- Job Role
- Monthly Income
- Years at Company
- Overtime Status
- Job Satisfaction
- Work-Life Balance
- Attrition Status

## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Executive Overview
This page provides a high-level summary of employee attrition, including total employees, attrition count, attrition rate, average monthly income, and average years at the company.

### 2. Employee Demographics
This page analyzes attrition by demographic groups such as age group, gender, marital status, and education field.

### 3. Job & Compensation Analysis
This page explores how attrition relates to income, tenure, job satisfaction, work-life balance, and job role.

## Key Metrics
- Total Employees
- Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate
- Average Monthly Income
- Average Years at Company
- Average Job Satisfaction

## Key Findings
- Overall employee attrition was 16.12%, with 237 employees leaving out of roughly 1,000 total employees.
- Employees with under 2 years of tenure had the highest attrition rate, suggesting early-career retention may be a major concern.
- The under $3K income group had the highest attrition rate, showing that lower monthly income may be associated with higher turnover.
- Attrition was highest among employees with the lowest work-life balance and job satisfaction scores.
- The Sales department had the highest attrition rate, while Research & Development had the highest total attrition count.
- Laboratory Technicians, Sales Executives, and Research Scientists had the highest attrition counts by job role.
- Employees working overtime accounted for a slightly larger share of attrition than employees not working overtime.
- The under 25 age group had the highest attrition rate, even though the largest employee groups were ages 25–34 and 35–44.

## Recommendations
Based on the dashboard findings, the company could consider:
- Reviewing overtime expectations and workload balance.
- Improving retention strategies for early-tenure employees.
- Evaluating compensation for lower-income employee groups.
- Monitoring departments and job roles with higher attrition rates.
- Improving employee satisfaction and work-life balance initiatives.
